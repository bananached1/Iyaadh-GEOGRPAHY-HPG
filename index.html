Untapped Resources

Fresh Water

Antarctica holds 70% of the world's fresh-water reserves, a critical resource as over 2.2 billion people lack adequate access to water today, with half the world's population living in water-stressed areas.

Oil & Natural Gas

Antarctica is estimated to harbor nearly 48 billion barrels of oil and 115 trillion cubic feet of natural gas, enough to fuel the world for nearly half a decade.

Precious Metals

The continent has vast amounts of high-value ores, including gold, coal, and chromium, which are crucial in most industrial processes.

Extraction Challenges

Infrastructure

With essentially no permanent population or existing infrastructure, building extraction facilities would require enormous investment in transportation, housing, and heating for workers in extreme conditions.

Cost

Building an oil-extraction complex in Antarctica would cost upwards of $40 billion, requiring a workforce of approximately 70,000 personnel over a 3-year period.

Legal Constraints

The Madrid Protocol prohibits mining and extraction of any resource in Antarctica, though ambiguities in the treaty could potentially be exploited through different interpretations.

Environmental Impact

Extraction operations risk complete annihilation of one of the most remarkable and fragile ecosystems on earth through inevitable oil spills and pollution.

Is Resource Extraction Justifiable?
